## Description
Discovered during verification of beads-workbench-90w.2. Expected: the redesigned 3-pane detail screen should use available vertical space well, with comments rendered as a visually secondary lower subsection inside the dominant Content pane. Actual: built-binary runtime captures at 120x34, 140x34, and 160x34 against the embedded fixture show description/notes/comments clustered in the upper half of Content with a large blank region below, so comments are not positioned as a lower subsection and the layout still feels vertically under-filled.

## Likely Root Cause
The Content pane currently renders sections top-down and then pads remaining height with blank lines, so short/medium content stays top-aligned and comments are never intentionally pushed lower inside the pane.

## Fix Direction
Preserve the accepted single Content-pane scroll model. Rebalance vertical allocation inside the Content pane by inserting adaptive spacer lines between upper content (details/notes) and the comments subsection when total content height is shorter than the available inner pane height. When content already fills/exceeds available height, insert no spacer and preserve current scroll behavior.

## Constraints
- Do not split Content into multiple focus panes or multiple independent scroll regions.
- Do not hardcode for a single terminal size such as 120x34.
- Keep comments visually secondary and lower in the Content pane when there is spare height.
- Preserve shared rendering/component reuse already introduced by beads-workbench-90w.1.

## Repro
1. `go build -o /tmp/bwb ./cmd/bwb`
2. `repoPath="$(mktemp -d)" && sh internal/testing/e2e/embeddedfixture/setup.sh "$repoPath" internal/testing/e2e/embeddedfixture/seed.json`
3. `python3 scripts/capture_bwb_screen.py --cwd "$repoPath" --width 120 --height 34 --startup-wait 1.2 --steps '0.2:3,0.2:CTRL+Q' -- -- env BD_NON_INTERACTIVE=1 /tmp/bwb`
4. Repeat at 140x34 and 160x34.

## Acceptance Criteria
- [ ] When total center-pane content is shorter than the available inner height, the comments subsection is pushed lower instead of remaining immediately under notes/body.
- [ ] When total center-pane content fills or exceeds available inner height, no spacer is inserted and scrolling behavior remains unchanged.
- [ ] Spacer calculation is adaptive to available pane height rather than hardcoded to one terminal size.
- [ ] Runtime captures at 120x34 and 160x34 show materially stronger vertical usage in the Content pane.
- [ ] Comments remain visually secondary and read as a lower subsection within Content.
