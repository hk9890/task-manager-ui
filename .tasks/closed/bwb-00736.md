## Discovered during

[[faif.5]] (run-against-scale audit, 2026-05-17). Surfaced when scale parity test reported `Ready: bwb=507 bd=100 delta=407` on the 590-issue scale fixture.

## Expected vs actual

Expected: `bd ready --json` without `--limit` returns all ready issues.

Actual: `bd ready --json` silently caps at 100 results regardless of corpus size — no error, no warning to stdout/stderr. `bd ready --explain --json` (used by `ReadyExplain` gateway method) bypasses this cap and returns all items.

The discrepancy is invisible below 100 ready issues. At scale (>100 ready) callers of `ReadyIssues()` see a silently truncated list and don't know it.

## Verification

Already confirmed at scale (590-issue scale fixture, 507 ready) — `bd ready --json | jq length` returns 100; `bd ready --explain --json | jq '.ready | length'` returns 507.

## Workaround

Pass `--limit 0` to `BdReady` (used in parity tests + smoke binary after this finding; landed in faif.5).

## Suggested fix

Upstream (bd): either change default to "no cap", or always emit a warning to stderr when the cap is engaged. The asymmetry between `bd ready` and `bd ready --explain` is itself confusing.

In bwb: `interface.go` ReadyExplain method now documents the asymmetry; scale-quirks appendix records the finding.
