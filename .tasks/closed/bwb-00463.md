## Status: Closed — not reproducible (PTY harness artifact)

This bug was filed during a 2026-05-24 PTY-driven exploration and **does not reflect a real defect**. Kept as a historical record so the same false-positive isn't refiled.

## Original Observation

`scripts/capture_bwb_screen.py` against the embedded fixture at 160x40 with `--repo beads`:

1. Send `Enter` on a board row → wait for `Detail:` (passes; detail opens for `bwf-2`).
2. Send `ESCAPE` → wait for `cols 1-3/4` (the unique board header) — **timed out at 3000 ms**.
3. Second `ESCAPE` had no effect.

The original filing concluded `Esc` was non-functional from detail mode — contradicting `docs/user-guide/key-bindings.md` line 19 ("esc — return from detail/search to browse, or dismiss toast state").

## Investigation

Unit/integration tests around the detail mode show `Esc` correctly returns to the board. Manual repro in a real terminal (not the PTY harness) likewise works. Several PTY-side root causes were identified:

- The harness's `capture_bwb_screen.py` was silently falling through unknown key-name strings to a literal send (fixed in znri.8). Some `ESCAPE` invocations during the session may have been sent as the literal string instead of `\x1b`.
- Detail mode's transition to board may emit a paint that races the harness's `wait-for-text` timeout window.

## Resolution

Closed `not-reproducible`. The real corrective action was to harden the PTY harness in znri.8 so future explorations don't repeat the false positive.
